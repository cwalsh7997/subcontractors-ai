Best Construction Project Management Software for Subcontractors (2026)

Find the right PM tool for your subcontracting business. Compare Procore, Buildertrend, CoConstruct, Subcontractors.ai, and Fieldwire based on pricing, features, and fit for subs.

Home › Blog › Construction PM Software

Subcontractors juggle complexity that most general contractors never face. You're bidding on multiple projects from different GCs, coordinating with other trades on tight schedules, tracking time and materials across job sites, and chasing late payments from general contractors. The right project management software can cut this chaos by 40-60%.

But most PM tools are built for GCs managing teams on their own projects. They have features subs don't need and miss features subs rely on daily. This guide compares the top options and explains why traditional PM software often misses the mark for subcontractors.

What Subcontractors Actually Need in PM Software

Before comparing tools, here's what matters most for subs:

  • Bid Management: Create and track multiple bids going to different GCs
  • Payment Tracking: Know which GC invoices are 30, 60, 90+ days overdue
  • Multi-Trade Coordination: See which other subs are on each job (scheduling conflicts)
  • Time & Materials Tracking: Quick invoicing from daily labor and material logs
  • GC Communication: Built-in messaging with project managers and field teams
  • Mobile Efficiency: Works offline at the job site (critical for trade crew leaders)
  • Simple Reporting: Profit per job, payment status, crew utilization

1. Procore

Best for: Large general contractors and mega-subs with 50+ employees.

Pricing: $100-400+/month per user (enterprise pricing available). Most subs find it overpriced for their scope.

Overview: Procore is the dominant platform in construction, with 2+ million active users and integration with most project financing and accounting systems. It offers comprehensive project management, budget tracking, RFI management, and document control. For a GC managing 20+ projects with large teams, it's the industry standard.

Pros for Subcontractors:

  • Industry standard (many GCs require subs to use it)
  • Excellent document management and collaboration
  • Strong mobile app for field teams
  • Integrates with major accounting software

Cons for Subcontractors:

  • Per-user pricing becomes expensive for small sub crews
  • Designed for GCs managing their own projects, not subs chasing bids
  • No built-in GC payment tracking or late-payment alerts
  • Steep learning curve for small teams
  • Requires significant administrative setup for each project

2. Buildertrend

Best for: Residential and mid-market contractors (both GCs and subs).

Pricing: $99-299/month. Unlimited users at higher tiers.

Overview: Buildertrend is purpose-built for residential construction. It focuses on project management, client communication, job costing, and crew scheduling. It's more affordable than Procore and easier to learn, making it popular with smaller GCs and residential subs.

Pros for Subcontractors:

  • More affordable than Procore (flat-rate plans, not per-user)
  • Good for residential framing, electrical, plumbing crews
  • Decent mobile app for field teams
  • Integrates with QuickBooks for accounting
  • Customer support is responsive

Cons for Subcontractors:

  • Minimal bid management features (assumes work is already secured)
  • No built-in GC payment tracking or late-payment alerts
  • Less robust for commercial/heavy-civil work
  • Limited integration with other platforms subs rely on
  • Not designed for subs working across multiple GCs

3. CoConstruct

Best for: Residential remodeling and custom home builders.

Pricing: $199-599/month. Designed for single contractors, not teams.

Overview: CoConstruct is specialized for residential remodelers. It emphasizes client communication, visual project management (photos and 3D), and cost tracking. It's particularly strong for estimating and presenting work to clients.

Pros for Subcontractors:

  • Excellent for residential remodeling subs
  • Strong client communication and photo/document features
  • Good cost tracking for materials and labor
  • Straightforward interface, easy to learn

Cons for Subcontractors:

  • Very residential-focused (not for commercial subs)
  • No multi-project bid management
  • Designed for contractors managing their own clients, not subs bidding to GCs
  • Limited collaboration with other trades
  • No GC payment tracking
  • Higher pricing for basic features compared to alternatives

4. Fieldwire

Best for: Field teams coordinating large commercial and heavy-civil projects.

Pricing: $99-299/month per account (scales with team size).

Overview: Fieldwire is a lighter-weight alternative to Procore, focusing on field coordination, RFI management, punch lists, and drawing markup. It's particularly strong for coordination between multiple trades on complex projects.

Pros for Subcontractors:

  • Excellent for multi-trade coordination on large projects
  • Strong field-first approach (works well offline on job sites)
  • Good drawing markup and RFI management
  • More affordable than Procore for team-based usage
  • Good for commercial and heavy-civil subs

Cons for Subcontractors:

  • Primarily a field coordination tool, not a comprehensive PM platform
  • No bid management or payment tracking
  • Doesn't integrate tightly with accounting/invoicing
  • Less robust for single-trade crew management
  • Designed to be used alongside another primary PM system
