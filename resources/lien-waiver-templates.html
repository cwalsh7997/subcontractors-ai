What is a Lien Waiver?

A lien waiver is a legal document that subcontractors and suppliers sign to give up their right to file a lien on a project. In exchange for payment (or promise of payment), you waive your ability to place a lien against the property if issues arise. It's a critical protection for general contractors and property owners — but it's equally important that you understand what you're signing away as a sub.

Most Common
Conditional Progress Waiver

Use after each invoice payment on an ongoing project.

  • Works for partial payments
  • Requires actual payment to process
  • Resets with each new payment
  • Protects your lien rights
Higher Risk
Unconditional Progress Waiver

Released without proof of payment — don't use this lightly.

  • Effective immediately
  • No conditions on payment
  • Waiver takes effect even if check bounces
  • Requires extreme caution
Project Closeout
Conditional Final Waiver

When your work is complete and you're getting final payment.

  • Last payment of the project
  • Conditional on receiving full amount
  • Includes retainage release
  • Releases all claims on project
Full Release
Unconditional Final Waiver

Complete release of all lien rights for the entire project.

  • Final waiver for full completion
  • No conditions on final payment
  • Complete release of claims
  • Use only after full payment

When to Use Each Type

Conditional: Most projects. You sign only after payment clears.

Unconditional: Rarely. Only if you fully trust the GC and have been paid before signing.

Critical Tip

Never sign a waiver before you're paid. A conditional waiver protects you — sign only after the check clears or you receive confirmed payment.
